# YieldPOS Product Summary And Website Plan

## Plain-English Product Summary

YieldPOS is a touchscreen point-of-sale system built for busy independent shops that need fast service, easy product control, reliable hardware, and clear end-of-day reporting. It is especially suited to fresh produce, grocery, deli, butcher, cafe, convenience, market, and mixed retail shops where staff need to sell by touch, barcode, PLU, weight, open price, special deal, or quick search.

The main idea is simple: the register screen is built for speed at the counter, while the admin screen gives the owner or manager control over products, staff, prices, keyboards, reports, receipts, hardware, backups, and multi-register setup.

YieldPOS is designed for shops that want practical control without being locked into a complicated cloud-only system. It can run from a local shop computer, keep the live shop database as the source of truth, work with common retail hardware, and optionally use cloud or local network syncing where needed.

## Who YieldPOS Is For

YieldPOS is a good fit for:

- Fruit and vegetable shops with weighable produce, PLUs, open-price items, and fast category keyboards.
- Grocery and specialty food stores with many products, barcodes, specials, and price tags.
- Delis, butchers, bakeries, florists, coffee counters, and mixed independent retailers.
- Shops that need one or more touchscreen registers.
- Shops that want staff PINs, cash drawer control, returns, receipt reprints, and end-of-day reports.
- Shops that use barcode scanners, receipt printers, cash drawers, scales, or Linkly EFTPOS terminals.
- Shops that want owner-managed product data rather than relying on a distant cloud system to overwrite local changes.

## Core Promise

YieldPOS helps a shop serve customers quickly, keep product and price data under control, and understand each trading day without drowning the owner in technical setup.

The selling points are:

- Fast touchscreen register for real shop counters.
- Product buttons, barcode scanning, PLU lookup, item search, and weighed sales.
- Flexible product, category, keyboard, deal, and staff management.
- Cash, EFTPOS, split payment, refunds, returns, receipts, and drawer control.
- Daily reports, sales analysis, cash drawer summaries, audit logs, and backups.
- Hardware-friendly setup for scanners, printers, scales, cash drawers, and Linkly EFTPOS.
- Local shop database as the main source of truth, with optional sync tools.

## Register Mode

Register Mode is the daily sales screen used by staff at the counter. It is built around touch speed, large product buttons, barcode scanning, and clear transaction controls.

### Touchscreen Selling

The register has a large, visual keyboard area with department keys, product keys, category pages, and function keys. Staff can tap items directly, scan barcodes, enter PLUs, search products, or use open-price buttons for items that do not have fixed prices.

The register is designed for fast service during busy periods:

- Large touch buttons for common departments and products.
- Visual product/category keys with images and clear labels.
- Category pages for fruit, vegetables, grocery, deli, meat, flowers, coffee, nuts, gas, bread, and other shop sections.
- Product pages for more detailed groups like apples, bananas, citrus, potatoes, onions, tomatoes, pumpkins, berries, melons, grapes, and other produce areas.
- Back buttons and page navigation for staff who are moving quickly.
- A cart panel that shows items, quantities, discounts, totals, tax, and payment status.
- A hidden scan input so barcode scanner entry goes straight into the sale flow.

### Product Entry Methods

YieldPOS supports several ways to add items to a sale:

- Barcode scan.
- PLU or code entry.
- Product search by name, barcode, or PLU.
- Direct touchscreen product button.
- Category browsing.
- Fixed-price item buttons.
- Open-price buttons where staff enter the price.
- Weighed items using a connected scale where available.
- Manual weight entry if required.
- Quantity entry and quantity adjustment.

This matters for shops with mixed selling styles. A grocery item may scan by barcode, bananas may be sold by weight, flowers may be open price, and a deli or special product may be entered from a touchscreen key.

### Cart And Sale Controls

The sale cart supports common counter workflows:

- Add products to the current sale.
- Increase or reduce quantities.
- Remove an item.
- Clear a sale.
- Apply item or whole-order discounts.
- Show subtotals, tax, discounts, and final total.
- Use return mode for refunds.
- Hold a sale and recall it later.
- Reprint recent receipts.
- View completed sales after payment.
- Clear the completed transaction and start fresh.

### Payments

YieldPOS supports practical payment flows for independent retail:

- Cash payment.
- EFTPOS/card payment.
- Manual EFTPOS accepted flow.
- Account payment option.
- Split payments.
- Cash rounding.
- Change calculation.
- Change breakdown guidance.
- Refund processing.
- Payment receipt handling.

The payment screens are designed to be clear for staff, with the total due visible, payment options available, and confirmation flows for completed sales.

### Linkly EFTPOS

YieldPOS includes Linkly EFTPOS support for shops using Linkly Cloud compatible payment terminals. The app includes tools for:

- Linkly Cloud credential setup.
- Pairing a terminal with a pairing code.
- Testing the connection.
- Purchase requests.
- Refund requests.
- Settlement.
- Payment polling.
- EFTPOS receipt data.

For a shop owner, the practical benefit is that card payments can be driven from the POS instead of staff manually typing totals into the EFTPOS terminal, where the hardware and merchant setup support it.

### Cash Drawer And Till Controls

YieldPOS includes cash drawer tools for the register:

- No sale / open drawer action.
- Set drawer float.
- Record pickups.
- Record drops.
- Track expected cash in drawer.
- Show cash sales, float, pickups, and drops.
- Add notes to cash drawer actions.
- Include drawer activity in reporting.

This gives the owner a clearer view of cash movement, not just sales totals.

### End Of Day

The End of Day flow helps staff count the drawer and close the trading day:

- Count denominations.
- Calculate counted cash total.
- Compare expected cash with counted cash.
- Record notes.
- Log drawer activity.
- Print an end-of-day style receipt/report.
- Connect with daily sales reporting.

This is useful for shops where closing is handled by different staff and the owner wants a consistent process.

### Returns And Refunds

YieldPOS includes a return workflow:

- Search completed sales.
- Filter or search past transactions.
- Choose the original sale.
- Select specific items to return.
- Add returned items to the cart as negative lines.
- Complete a cash or card refund.
- Mark transaction status appropriately.

The goal is to make returns traceable rather than just entering random negative amounts.

### Item Search And Price Check

Staff can search for products while serving:

- Search by name.
- Search by barcode.
- Search by PLU.
- View product results quickly.
- Add a selected product to cart.
- Check product prices without committing to a sale.

This is helpful when a product is not on the main keyboard, when a barcode is missing, or when a customer asks for a price.

### Staff Login

Register Mode supports staff PIN login:

- Staff can log in with a PIN.
- The active staff member is shown on screen.
- Sales, drawer actions, discounts, voids, and audits can be associated with staff.
- Admin/manager roles can control access to sensitive actions.

This gives the shop more accountability without slowing down service.

## Network, Sync, And Multi-Register Use

YieldPOS includes tools for shops that need more than one machine.

### Local Network Mode

The Network section supports a main-server/client-register model:

- Admin machine can act as the main server.
- Register machines can connect as clients.
- Connected registers can be viewed.
- LAN sync can be restarted.
- Client register can sync now.
- Network discovery tools are included.
- Network diagnostics check interfaces, port, discovered servers, subnet scan, and firewall status.

For a shop, the simple story is: one main computer owns the shop data, and extra registers can connect over the local network.

### Cloud Sync

YieldPOS includes optional Supabase cloud sync settings:

- Supabase URL.
- Supabase key.
- Cloud backup/sync connection.
- Manual pull where intentionally used.
- Push-style sync for local changes.

The important shop-facing wording should be careful: YieldPOS can support optional cloud backup/sync, but the live shop database remains the authority for the shop's products, prices, keyboard, deals, staff, and settings unless the owner intentionally pulls remote data.

## Data Safety And Reliability

YieldPOS is designed to avoid the common frustration where a shop changes a product or price and the system silently changes it back.

The shop-facing message should be:

- The live shop database is the main source of truth.
- Product and price edits are meant to stay edited.
- The bundled starter database is only for first setup or intentional reset.
- Backups can be created and restored.
- Logs and health screens help diagnose problems.
- Optional cloud and LAN sync tools exist, but they should not quietly overwrite shop data in normal use.

## Typical Shop Workflows

### Morning Setup

1. Staff open Register Mode.
2. Staff log in with PIN.
3. Drawer float is entered or confirmed.
4. Hardware indicators show scanner, scale, and other connected devices where available.
5. The shop begins selling.

### Serving A Customer

1. Scan products, tap product buttons, search items, or enter open prices.
2. Use scale weight or manual weight entry for weighed items.
3. Apply any discount or deal if needed.
4. Take cash, card, account, or split payment.
5. Print or skip receipt depending on the shop setting.
6. Show change and clear the completed sale for the next customer.

### Price Change

1. Open Admin Mode or use the price-change workflow where enabled.
2. Search product or tap the keyboard product.
3. Edit price, unit, PLU, or open-price setting.
4. Save once.
5. The register uses the updated product data.

### Adding A New Product

1. Go to Products.
2. Add name, barcode/PLU, category, unit, price, tax, and image if needed.
3. Optionally add it to a keyboard button.
4. Optionally add it to a deal or price tag queue.

### Running A Promotion

1. Go to Deals.
2. Create a deal name and pricing.
3. Add eligible products.
4. Confirm every deal product has a PLU.
5. Activate the deal.
6. Staff can sell without memorising the offer.

### End Of Day

1. Run End of Day from the register.
2. Count cash by denomination.
3. Record notes if required.
4. Print the report.
5. Review reports, payment methods, and drawer totals in Admin.

- "Use real retail hardware at the counter."
- "Your shop data stays under your control."

Avoid:

- Too much technical language.
- Long software architecture explanations.
- Generic cloud-software claims.
- Abstract productivity wording that does not connect to the counter.
